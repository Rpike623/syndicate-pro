Document Generation: OAs, PPMs, and Sub Docs in 30 Seconds

8 min readMarch 2026Feature Walkthrough

Every syndication deal requires a stack of legal documents: an Operating Agreement, a Private Placement Memorandum, subscription documents, and capital call notices. Most sponsors pay their attorney $5,000-$15,000 per deal for these documents — which is money well spent for the final versions. But the first draft? That doesn't need to cost $5,000.

deeltrack generates first-draft legal documents from your deal profile in about 30 seconds. You fill in the deal terms once, and the documents write themselves.

Operating Agreement Generator

The OA is the governing document of your syndication entity. It defines:

  • Entity structure (LLC name, formation state, registered agent)
  • Capital contributions and ownership percentages
  • Waterfall distribution structure (pref rate, promote, catch-up)
  • Management authority and GP responsibilities
  • Transfer restrictions and buyout provisions
  • Dissolution and exit provisions

deeltrack pulls all of this from your deal profile. Entity name, pref rate, promote structure, investor commitments — it's already in the system. Click "Generate OA" and you get a formatted document with every variable filled in.

Important disclaimer:

Generated documents are first drafts — starting points for your attorney, not replacements for legal counsel. Every OA should be reviewed by a securities attorney before execution. deeltrack saves you the $2,000 your attorney would charge to draft from scratch by giving them a structured document to review and mark up instead.

AI Document Parsing

The reverse workflow is just as useful: you receive a broker's Offering Memorandum and need to extract the deal terms into structured data.

Parse Broker OMs

Upload an OM PDF and deeltrack's AI extracts:

  • Property name, location, and type
  • Unit count, square footage, year built
  • Asking price, price per unit/sq ft
  • NOI, cap rate, projected returns
  • Debt terms (if included)

The extracted data can be used to create a new deal profile — saving you 15 minutes of manual data entry per deal.

Parse Document Variables

Upload an existing OA or legal document and deeltrack extracts every variable: entity names, dates, dollar amounts, percentages, addresses, and defined terms. This is useful when you're onboarding an existing deal that has documents but no structured data.

AI Document Review

Once you have both a deal profile and documents, deeltrack's AI can compare them:

  • Matches — terms in the document that align with your deal data (pref rate matches, entity name matches)
  • Mismatches — discrepancies between the document and your deal profile (document says 7% pref but deal is set to 8%)
  • Warnings — clauses that might need attention (unusual transfer restrictions, missing dissolution provisions)

This is particularly valuable during due diligence when you're reviewing documents from the seller or a third party. Instead of reading 40 pages line by line, you get a summary of what matches, what doesn't, and what's missing.

Capital Call Notices

When you issue a capital call, deeltrack generates the notice with:

  • Purpose of the call (acquisition closing, capital improvement, operating shortfall)
  • Per-investor amounts (pro-rata based on commitment)
  • Wire instructions (pulled from your settings)
  • Due date and payment instructions

The AI can also draft the body text — explaining to your investors why you're calling capital and what the funds will be used for. You review the draft, adjust the tone, and send.

Investor Update Drafts

The quarterly investor update is one of the most time-consuming GP tasks. deeltrack's AI drafting tool takes a different approach: instead of generating generic sunshine text, it starts from your bullet points.

  1. Type 3-5 bullet points about what happened this quarter ("occupancy dropped to 89%", "completed roof replacement", "negotiated new property management contract")
  2. The AI expands them into a professional quarterly update — with the right tone, structure, and level of detail
  3. It doesn't sugarcoat. If you say occupancy dropped, the draft acknowledges it and explains the recovery plan.

The result is a polished update in 30 seconds that would have taken 45 minutes to write from scratch — and it sounds like you, not like a chatbot.

Document Auto-Categorization

Upload any document and the AI categorizes it: legal, financial, environmental, insurance, tax, inspection, etc. It suggests a standardized filename and tags for searchability. This keeps your deal room organized without manual filing.
