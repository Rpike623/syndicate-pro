AI-Drafted Investor Updates: From Bullet Points to Polished Quarterly Reports

5 min readMarch 2026AI Tools

Quarterly investor updates are the single most important communication between a GP and their LPs. They set expectations, build trust, demonstrate transparency, and — when done well — make investors eager to re-invest in your next deal.

They also take forever to write. A well-crafted quarterly update for a single deal can take 45 minutes to an hour: gather the metrics, write the operations summary, address the capex, craft the outlook, and explain the distribution. Multiply by 5 deals, and you've lost an entire day.

deeltrack's AI drafting tool cuts that to about 30 seconds per deal.

How It Works

  1. Select the deal — the AI pulls in occupancy, rent, and financial data from the deal profile
  2. Type your bullet points — 3-5 things that happened this quarter in plain English
  3. Click "AI Draft" — get a fully structured quarterly update in ~6 seconds
  4. Review and edit — adjust tone, add personal touches, finalize

Example: Input → Output

Your bullet points: • Occupancy dropped to 89% after 6 move-outs in January
• Completed full roof replacement ($180K, under budget)
• Negotiated new PM contract saving $2K/month
• Next distribution expected April
AI-generated draft (abbreviated): "Dear Investors, We are providing your Q1 2026 update for Riverside Flats. This quarter presented several operational challenges which we are addressing proactively. Occupancy declined to 89% following six move-outs in January... We completed a full roof replacement at $180,000, coming in under our budgeted amount... On the property management front, we negotiated a new contract that will save approximately $2,000 per month going forward... We anticipate the next distribution in April..."

The AI doesn't generate generic sunshine text. It starts from your bullet points and expands them into professional prose. If you say occupancy dropped, the draft acknowledges it honestly — not buries it under optimistic language.

Honest by design:

We specifically built the AI prompt to avoid sugarcoating. Bad news delivered honestly builds more trust than bad news hidden behind corporate optimism. Your LPs are sophisticated — they can handle the truth, and they respect you more for delivering it clearly.

The Structure

Every AI-drafted update follows a standard structure:

  • Greeting — professional opening that sets the context
  • Operations — occupancy, leasing, market conditions
  • Capital expenditures — what was spent on improvements
  • Outlook — what to expect next quarter
  • Distribution note — when the next distribution is expected

This structure is consistent across updates, which means your LPs get used to where information lives. They can scan to the "Outlook" section without reading the whole thing — just like they would with any well-organized report.

What It's Not

The AI draft is a starting point. It's not a final product you send without reading. You should:

  • Review the tone and adjust for your voice
  • Add personal context the AI couldn't know
  • Verify that financial figures are correct
  • Include any investor-specific notes if needed

Think of it as having a sharp analyst write the first draft. You'd always review their work — but you wouldn't write it from scratch yourself when they can do 80% of it in seconds.
