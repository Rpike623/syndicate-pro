Getting Started

Quick Setup (5 minutes)

  1. Go to Settings and enter your firm name, GP name, and contact info.
  2. Set your default waterfall terms (Preferred Return, GP Promote) in Deal Defaults.
  3. Optionally add your wire instructions so investors know where to send funds.
  4. Optionally configure LP Portal Branding (logo, colors) for a white-labeled investor experience.
  5. Create your first deal via New Deal or Import from Excel.
  6. Add investors, generate invite links, and start raising capital!

The Investment Lifecycle

deeltrack manages the full flow from deal creation to funded investment:

  1. Create Deal — Enter property details, financial terms, and waterfall structure.
  2. Send Invite — Generate a unique deal link and email it to potential investors.
  3. LP Reviews & Commits — Investor sees deal terms, enters their commitment amount.
  4. GP Approves — Review pending commitments in the Commitment Queue.
  5. Send Sub Docs — Generate e-sign subscription agreement for the investor.
  6. LP Signs — Investor reviews deal-specific terms and electronically signs.
  7. Wire & Fund — Investor sends wire, GP confirms receipt. Deal is funded.
  8. Operate & Distribute — Post distributions, send K-1s, share updates.

Managing Deals

Creating a New Deal

  1. Click New Deal in the sidebar or from the Deals page.
  2. Fill in property name, location, asset type, and financial targets (raise, IRR, equity multiple).
  3. Set the waterfall structure: preferred return rate, GP promote percentage, and waterfall type (pref, catch-up, or simple split).
  4. Save the deal. It appears on your dashboard and deals list.

Importing Existing Deals from Excel

  1. Go to Import Deals and click "Download Template."
  2. Fill in the Deals sheet with your properties. Required: Deal Name. Optional: type, location, raise, IRR, etc.
  3. Fill in the Investors sheet with LP details and link them to deals by name.
  4. Fill in the Distributions sheet with historical distribution data.
  5. Upload the Excel file. Preview the data, then click "Import All."

Deal Statuses

Sourcing — Early stage, evaluating the opportunity.
LOI — Letter of Intent submitted.
Due Diligence — Under contract, conducting DD.
Raising — Actively raising capital from investors.
Operating — Deal closed, property is operating.
Closed — Exited / disposed.

Managing Investors

Adding Investors

Three ways to add investors:

  1. Manual: Investors page → Add Investor. Enter name, email, phone.
  2. Invite Link: From a deal page, click "Invite Link." LP creates their own account when they commit.
  3. Excel Import: Bulk import investors via the Import Deals template.

Investor Accreditation

Investors can self-certify as accredited directly in their portal. They select their qualification method (income, net worth, professional cert, entity, or knowledgeable employee), check a legal attestation, and submit. Certification is valid for 90 days and auto-expires. The dashboard warns you when investors' certifications are expiring.

Linking Investors to Deals

  1. On a Deal Detail page, click "Add Investor."
  2. Select the investor, enter committed amount and ownership percentage.
  3. Or: the investor commits via an invite link, and deeltrack auto-links them when you approve.

Running Distributions

Distribution Command Center

  1. Go to Distribution Command Center.
  2. Select a deal and enter the total amount to distribute.
  3. The waterfall engine calculates per-investor splits: preferred return first, then excess split per your deal terms.
  4. Review the pro-forma ledger showing each investor's payout.
  5. Click "Post to Ledger" to finalize. Investors are automatically notified via email.

QuickBooks Export

Go to QuickBooks Export to generate journal entries (IIF or CSV format) for distributions, capital calls, management fees, and acquisition fees. Import directly into QuickBooks Desktop or Online.

Commitment Queue

Managing Investor Commitments

  1. When an LP commits via an invite link, it appears in your Commitment Queue.
  2. Approve — auto-links the investor to the deal with their committed amount.
  3. Send Sub Docs — generates a signing link. LP receives email to e-sign.
  4. LP Signs — subscription agreement with deal-specific terms.
  5. Confirm Wire — enter wire receipt details. Commitment marked as funded.

Status flow: Pending → Approved → Docs Sent → Signed → Funded

Documents & K-1s

Deal Room

Upload and share documents per deal. Go to a deal → "Deal Room" action. Documents are accessible to linked investors through their portal.

K-1 Generation

  1. Go to K-1 Generator.
  2. Select deal, tax year, and investor.
  3. Enter income/loss allocations (ordinary income, rental income, capital gains, Section 199A).
  4. Generate and send to investor. K-1s appear in the LP's Tax Center.

Operating Agreement Generator

Go to OM Builder to create offering memorandums. The Tools page has an OA Generator that creates operating agreements with full §704 safe harbor allocation language, waterfall provisions, and state-specific clauses. Always have legal counsel review before use.

LP Portal

What Your Investors See

Each investor gets a secure portal at deeltrack.com/portal.html where they can:

  • View all their investments across your deals (and other GPs if applicable)
  • See distributions received, with full history
  • View and sign subscription documents
  • Access deal room documents and K-1s
  • Self-certify accreditation status
  • Update their profile: tax info (SSN/EIN), banking details for wire transfers
  • Submit wire confirmations after signing sub docs

White-Label Branding

Go to Settings → LP Portal Branding to customize the portal with your firm's name, logo, accent color, and favicon. Check "Hide deeltrack branding" for a fully white-labeled experience. Your investors will see your brand, not deeltrack's.

Multi-GP Support

If an investor invests with multiple GPs on deeltrack, their portal automatically aggregates across all firms. Deals are tagged with the GP name. No configuration needed — it works automatically when an investor is linked to deals in multiple orgs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my data secure?
Yes. All data is stored in Google Cloud Firestore with per-organization security rules. Sensitive fields (SSN/EIN, bank account numbers, routing numbers) are encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM. Data in transit is encrypted via HTTPS/TLS.
Is the e-signature legally binding?
Our electronic signature complies with the Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (E-SIGN Act, 15 U.S.C. §7001). Each signature captures the signer's name, timestamp, timezone, and browser information. A full-text snapshot of the signed agreement is stored immutably in Firestore at the moment of signing.
Can investors see other investors' data?
No. Firestore security rules enforce strict data isolation. Investors can only see their own records, the deals they're linked to, and distributions where they are a recipient. They cannot see other investors' committed amounts, personal information, or financial data.
How does the waterfall calculation work?
deeltrack supports three waterfall structures: Preferred Return (pref only, no catch-up), European Catch-Up (pref → GP catch-up → split), and Simple Split (no pref, straight split). The Distribution Command Center calculates per-investor splits based on your deal's waterfall terms, preferred return rate, and each investor's ownership percentage.
Can I use deeltrack for multiple funds/entities?
Yes. Each deal operates independently with its own investors, waterfall, and distributions. You can manage as many deals as you need from a single account. If you have separate GP entities, contact us about multi-org setup.
Does deeltrack replace my CPA or attorney?
No. deeltrack is a software tool that helps you organize, calculate, and manage your syndication operations. All generated documents (operating agreements, K-1s, offering memorandums) should be reviewed by qualified legal and tax professionals before use. deeltrack is not a broker-dealer, investment adviser, or placement agent.
How do I export my data?
Deals, investors, and distributions can be exported via the Export button on each page (CSV format). Journal entries for QuickBooks are available in IIF and CSV format from the QuickBooks Export page. K-1s can be exported as PDFs.
What happens when my trial ends?
After the 90-day free trial, GP-facing pages will prompt you to subscribe. Your data is preserved — nothing is deleted. Investor portals remain accessible (investors are never gated). Subscribe at any time to resume full access.
